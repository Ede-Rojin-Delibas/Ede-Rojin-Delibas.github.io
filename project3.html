Customer Churn Prediction & Retention Strategy

Machine learning pipeline to predict customer churn and drive retention

1. Introduction

Customer retention is one of the most critical challenges for subscription-based businesses. Acquiring new customers is significantly more expensive than retaining existing ones, making churn prediction a high-impact data science problem.

In this project, I developed a machine learning pipeline to predict customer churn and extract actionable insights to help businesses reduce customer loss and improve retention strategies.

2. Problem Statement

The company is experiencing customer churn but lacks visibility into:

  • Which customers are likely to leave
  • Why customers churn
  • How to proactively prevent churn

Without this knowledge, the company risks revenue loss and inefficient marketing efforts.

3. Objectives

  • Predict whether a customer will churn (classification problem)
  • Identify key drivers behind customer churn
  • Provide actionable business insights
  • Build a model that prioritizes recall to minimize missed churn cases

4. Data Source

  • Dataset: IBM Telco Customer Churn Dataset
  • Features include:
    • Customer demographics (gender, senior citizen, etc.)
    • Account information (contract type, tenure)
    • Services used (internet, streaming, security)
    • Billing & payment behavior

5. Methodology

5.1 Data Understanding & EDA

Analyzed feature distributions and relationships with churn. Identified key patterns:

  • Month-to-month contracts → high churn
  • Electronic check users → high churn
  • Lack of security services → higher churn

These patterns revealed that customers with flexible contracts and limited service engagement show significantly higher churn propensity. Understanding these relationships guided our feature engineering strategy.

Internet Service & Churn Relationship: Fiber optic users demonstrate notably higher churn rates compared to DSL and no-internet customers, indicating a potential quality or pricing dissatisfaction issue.

5.2 Feature Engineering

Created meaningful features to improve model performance:

  • TotalServices → number of services used
  • SecurityScore → security-related service usage
  • TenureGroup → customer lifecycle segmentation
  • MonthlyChargeGroup → pricing segmentation

Customer Tenure & Churn Pattern: New customers (first 1-6 months) exhibit the highest churn risk, while tenure increases sharply reduce churn probability. This emphasizes the importance of early-stage customer engagement and satisfaction.

5.3 Data Preprocessing

  • Handled missing values (TotalCharges)
  • Converted categorical variables:
    • Binary encoding (Yes/No → 0/1)
    • One-hot encoding for multi-class features
    • Feature scaling for numerical variables

Contract Type & Churn Relationship: Month-to-month contracts show 3x higher churn rates compared to 1-2 year contracts, validating the hypothesis that commitment level directly influences customer retention.

5.4 Modeling

Built and compared multiple models:

  • Logistic Regression (baseline)
  • Random Forest
  • XGBoost

Applied cross-validation, class imbalance handling, and threshold tuning for recall optimization.

6. Evaluation & Results

Cross Validation (Baseline)

Mean F1 Score: ~0.58 | Stable but moderate performance

Final Model Performance (XGBoost)

Metric Score
Accuracy 0.76
Precision (Churn) 0.54
Recall (Churn) 0.83
F1 Score 0.65

Key Insight: The model successfully identifies 83% of customers who are likely to churn. This is critical because missing a churned customer = direct revenue loss.

7. Key Business Insights

High Churn Drivers

  • Fiber Optic Internet Users → Higher expectations → dissatisfaction risk
  • Month-to-Month Contracts → No long-term commitment
  • Electronic Check Payments → Friction in payment experience
  • Lack of Security Services → Lower engagement & trust
  • New Customers (Low Tenure) → Early-stage churn risk

8. Business Value

Churn Risk Segmentation

  • High-risk customers identified in advance
  • Personalized retention strategies

Recommended Actions by Risk Level

Risk Level Action
High Direct outreach, special offers
Medium Email campaigns
Low Passive monitoring

Impact: Reduced customer churn | Increased customer lifetime value (CLTV) | More efficient marketing strategies
